on the discipline “Computer science” are presented.

Informatics in technical systems

The discipline "Computer science" is focused on studying by students of theoretical bases of computer science, the information
and information interaction. Includes consideration of arithmetic, logic bases of digital automatic machines, tendencies of development of the architecture of information systems, and also hardware and software modern PC. The discipline has a practical orientation
on the formation of abilities to operate with numbers in various notations and skills of simplification of logic expressions by working out of block diagrammes of logic units.

The working program of the discipline "Computer science" is developed according to operating educational standards and provides preparation of the diplomaed experts in directions 651900 – “Automation and management”, 654700 – “Information systems”
on specialities 210100 – “Management and computer science in technical systems”, 071900 – “Information systems and technologies”.

The discipline "Computer science" is the base to studying of the subsequent disciplines: "Programming", “Structures of the data”, “Interfaces of information systems”, “Corporate information systems”, “Theory of information processes and systems”.

 

Goals and tasks of the discipline:

1. Studying of the conceptual basis of the theory of computer science and the information.

2. Studying of the arithmetic and logic bases of digital automatic machines (COMPUTER).

3. Studying of the genesis and evolution of information systems architecture.

4. Studying of the basic elements and devices of modern PC architecture.

5. Formation of abilities to carry out the elementary arithmetic operations under numbers in various scales of notations.

6. Formation of the skills of transformation and simplification of logic expressions.

7.  Studying of the receptions providing effective work with the interface and formation of practical skills in administrating of operating system Windows 98/2000/XP, and also uses of standard applications.

8. Studying of Word applications, Excel a package of applied programs MS Office 2000/XP.

 

According to requirements to level of development of the discipline students should:

1. Know the basic concepts of the theory of the information, feature of its representation and processing.

2. Be able to transform numbers from one scale of notation to another, and also carry out the elementary operations over numbers.

3. Be able to use logic algebra laws for simplification of logic expressions.

4. Know about genesis and evolution of the COMPUTER architecture.

5. Know the nobility of feature of information systems and to know about generations of evolution of the COMPUTER.

6. Know architecture and principles of functioning of elements of the classical (Princeton) COMPUTER (under John Fon Neumann).

7. Know about architecture of modern COMPUTERS, its internal and external elements.

8. Know appointment of the basic external (peripheral) devices modern PC.

9. Know study the COMPUTER software: operating system MS Windows 98/2000/XP and a package of applied programs MS Office 97/2000.

 

 

The maintenance of the working program of the discipline “Computer science”

The list of sections of the discipline

Unit 1. Theoretical basis of Informatics and information

Module 1. Informational society, informational crisis, the information

Informational society: Concept, the basic features. Information crisis (information revolution): historical kinds, a role, value, influence. Society information: concept, influence. Information resources, products and services in the information market.

 

Module 2. Theoretical bases of computer science

Computer science: concept, genesis, a subject and tasks (functions). Structure and tasks of computer science: manufacture branch, fundamental science, applied discipline, hardware, software, brainware.

 

Module 3. Theoretical bases of the information

The information: concept, features, kinds, adequacy forms (syntactic, semantic, pragmatical). Information classification (on occurrence time, on stability, on a way of use and availability). The information: measures and quantity (syntactic, semantic and pragmatical approaches). The information: consumer indicators of quality (representativity, pithiness, reliability, availability, an urgency, efficiency, accuracy). The information: external and internal properties. A signal: concept, kinds. Quantization of a continuous (analogue) signal: concept, main kinds (on time, on level), an error (error) of transformation. The information and the data: features of representation
of the information in the COMPUTER.

 

Unit 2. Arithmetic bases of digital automatic machines (COMPUTER)

Module 4. Information interaction and information representation in the COMPUTER

Informational interaction: concept, structures, principles. Coding of information: concept, classification ways of representation (coding) of the information. Direct, return and additional codes of number.

 

Module 5. Notations and the elementary arithmetic operations under numbers

Notation: concept, main kinds (not item, item), the comparative characteristic. A notation: concept, item notations (classification by the basis). Binary, octal, decimal and hexadecimal notations: classification of rules of transfer of numbers (for the whole and fractional parts of number). Binary, octal, decimal and hexadecimal notations: rules of performance of the elementary arithmetic operations.

 

Unit 3. Logic bases of digital automatic machines (COMPUTER)

Module 6. The basic logic operations (functions)

The basic logic functions (operations): concept, ways of representation (analytical, tabular, the functional-logic scheme, the electric basic scheme, the time diagram). Logic operation (function): concept, kinds (simple, difficult, a performance priority). Operation
of inversion, logic negation (NOT): analytical (formula), tabular (the validity table), schematic (a conditional-graphic designation
of inversion on the logic scheme and the electric basic scheme of the inverter). Operation of conjunction, logic multiplication (AND): analytical (formula), tabular (the validity table), schematic (an is conditional-graphic designation of conjunction on the logic scheme and the electric basic scheme conjunctor). Operation of a disjunction, logic addition (OR): Analytical (formula), tabular (the validity table), schematic (a conditional-graphic designation of a disjunction on the logic scheme and the electric basic scheme disjunctor).

 

Module 7. Laws of algebra of logic

Laws of logic algebras (algebra of logic): concept, appointment, essence, kinds (commutative, combinative, distributive, the law of zero set, the law of universal set, the repetition law, inversion laws). Sequence of synthesis of logic units.

 

Unit 4. Genesis and tendencies of the development of the architecture of information systems

Module 8. Genesis and evolution of information systems architecture

System: concept, kinds (social, PC, telecommunication, information), structural elements, the purposes of functioning, features. COMPUTER generations: the comparative characteristic (on the period of time, element base, integration level, the basic devices
of input/output, software, OS operating mode, use purposes).

 

Module 9. Classical (Princeton) architecture of the COMPUTER

Classical (Princeton) architecture of the COMPUTER (after John Fon Neumann): concept, functioning principles. Structural elements
of classical architecture of the COMPUTER: appointment, interrelation in the cycle of functioning. Elements of neoclassical
architecture of the COMPUTER. Internal memory: concept, appointment, addressing structure offered by IBM and Microsoft
(within the limits of standardization). External memory: concept, appointment, physical and logic structure of magnetic disks.
A control mean: appointment, structure, action principle. Arithmetic-logic devices: appointment, structure, action principle.

 

Unit 5. Basic elements and devices of the modern PC architecture

Module 10. Internal elements and devices of the modern COMPUTERS

Classification of the modern COMPUTERS: to destination, in the sizes and functionality, on design features of construction. Architecture of the modern COMPUTERS: concept, basic principles. Internal structure of PC: concept, structure and the short characteristic of elements: a system board, the central processor, memory. A system board: concept, appointment, topology. Slots (buses, interfaces) expansions: concept, classification, appointment, communication topology, a short characteristic. The central processor: concept, appointment, structure, parameters and the comparative characteristic of generations. Internal memory: concept, kinds, appointment, classification, the comparative characteristic and evolution of microcircuits of modules of memory.

 

Module 11. Stores of the information at the base of architecture of the modern COMPUTERS

Information stores: concept, kinds, features of realisation, the short characteristic (streamers, disk stores, stores on flash cards). Physical and logic structure of magnetic disks of disk stores. Formatting of floppy disc drive (FDD) and hard disc drive (HDD): concept, characteristics, features. Interfaces of hard disc drives: concept, versions, classification, the characteristic (the interface of low level ATAPI and intellectual multipurpose interface SCSI). Laser stores on optical disks of CD-ROM and DVD-ROM: concept, classification, parameters, features.

 

Module 12. External elements and devices of the modern COMPUTERS

The architecture of the COMPUTER on the basis of Intel Pentium4 processor: concept, topology, features of realisation. External structure PC: concept, structure and a short characteristic of elements (the system block, the display, the keyboard, the mouse,
the peripheral equipment). The system block and power unit of PC: concept, appointment, technical characteristics, realization versions. Peripheral equipment of PC: concept, kinds of devices, appointment, short characteristics (devices of input/output and interactive communication). Display of PC: concept, appointment, classification, technical characteristics. The printer: concept, appointment, classification, technologies of the print. The keyboard and manipulators of PC: concept, appointment, classification, technical characteristics.

 

Unit 6. Software of the modern PC (it is studied on a laboratory in practical work)

Module 13. Operating system MS Windows 98/2000/XP

The interaction interface. The task manager. The starting menu. The program "Explorer". The Control panel. Standard applications. Multitasking, resident and DOS-applications.

 

Module 14. A package of applied programs MS Office 97/2000: Text editor Word

Work bases in word-processor Word. Formatting of symbols and paragraphs. Formatting of structurally difficult documents.
The expanded possibilities of formatting of documents Word. Work with graphic objects in word-processor Word. Creation
of documents of merge, the correspondence and forms in word-processor Word. Managements of big editions and reviewing
in word-processor Word.

 

Module 15. A package of applied programs MS Office 97/2000: System of spreadsheets Excel

The general data on tabular processor Excel. Features of data input and formatting in tabular processor Excel. Work sheet formatting
in tabular processor Excel. Work sheet editing in tabular processor Excel. Realisation of analytically-numerical methods of the decision
of problems in Excel. Realisation of analytically-numerical methods of the decision of problems in Excel. Search of decisions
and consolidation of the data.
